THE LIGHTNING

1. Cloud Electrification by Friction

  • Updraft: Warm air lifts light droplets and crystals.
  • Origin: Droplets freeze onto crystals → graupel is born.
  • Collision: Falling graupel hits rising ice crystals.
  • Charge (−): Graupel gains electrons and falls to the base.
  • Charge (+): Crystals lose electrons and rise to the top.

2. Ground Electrification by Induction

  • Repulsion: The negative cloud base repels electrons on the ground.
  • Movement: Electrons flee to deeper soil layers.
  • Surface: The ground is left with a Positive (+) charge.

3. The Electric Discharge (Lightning)

  • Stepped Leader (-): Descends in zigzags seeking the easiest path.
  • Upward Streamer (+): Rises from the ground to complete the circuit.
  • Connection: Once they meet, the main strike follows their combined path.
